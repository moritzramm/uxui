UX/UI Workflow Draft

Empathize

Gain an understanding of the problem you are trying to solve. Consult experts, find out about the area of concern through observing, engage and empathize with people to understand their experiences and motivations, immerse yourself in the physical environment, gain a deeper personal understanding of the issue.

Methodology and Tools

  • Surveys
  • Stakeholder Interviews
  • User Interviews
  • Focus Groups
  • Online Research
  • Competitor Research
  • Business & Competitive Analysis
  • Lean Survey Canvas
  • Feature Comparison

Define

Put together the information you have created and gathered during the Empathise stage. Analyse your observations and synthesise them in order to define the core problems that you have identified up to this point. Define the problem as a problem statement in a human-centred manner.

Methodology and Tools

  • Affinity Diagram
  • Empathy Map
  • User Persona(s)
  • Story Board
  • User Journey
  • Problem Statement
  • 5 'W's - Who, What, Where, When and Why
  • HowMightWe Statement
  • Pain Pointe

Ideate

Start generating ideas! You’ve grown to understand your users and their needs in the Empathise stage, and you’ve analysed and synthesised your observations in the Define stage, and ended up with a human-centered problem statement. Start to "think outside the box".

Methodology and Tools

  • Crazy 8
  • MoSCoW Method
  • Round Robin
  • Worst Ideas
  • HowMightWe Statement
  • Brand Attributes
  • Information Architecture
  • Sitemap
  • Card Sorting
  • Sitemap review
  • User Flow

Prototype

Produce inexpensive, scaled down versions of the product or specific features, so one can investigate the problem solutions generated in the previous stage. It's an experimental phase, the aim is to identify the best possible solution for each of the problems identified.

Methodology and Tools

  • Low-Fidelity Prototype
  • Low-Fidelity Testing
  • Iterations
  • Mid-Fidelity Prototype
  • Mid-Fidelity Testing
  • Iterations
  • Hi-Fidelity Prototype
  • Microinteractions

Testing

Rigorously test the complete product using the best solutions identified during the prototyping phase. It's an iterative process, the results are used to redefine one or more problems and inform the understanding of the users, the conditions of use, how people think, behave, and feel.
